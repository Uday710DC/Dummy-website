A Phase 3 study, three things to know.

A summary of what this study is testing, who it's for, and how it differs from many clinical trials in CIDP and neurology.

💉
PHASE 3
What's being tested

KIg10 — an investigational IVIG 10% therapy

An intravenous immune globulin product evaluated as a maintenance therapy for adults with confirmed CIDP, comparing two dosing strengths.

  • Two dose groups: 0.5 g/kg and 1.0 g/kg
  • Infusion every 3 weeks
  • Both groups receive active treatment
  • Total ~8–10 months participation
👥
ADULTS
Who it's for

Adults with confirmed CIDP, currently on treatment

For adults whose CIDP has been managed with IVIG, SCIG, corticosteroids, or other standard therapy, and has been stable in recent months.

  • Ages 18 and older
  • Diagnosed by a neurologist (EAN/PNS 2021)
  • Currently on CIDP treatment
  • Stable for at least 12 weeks

Important: both groups receive active medication.

Unlike many clinical studies, this Phase 3 trial does not use a placebo. Participants are randomly assigned to receive either 0.5 g/kg or 1.0 g/kg of KIg10 — both clinically meaningful doses of active IVIG. If symptoms worsen during the study, a rescue dose (2.0 g/kg) of active treatment is provided.

⚠ Important disclosure

Before the study drug begins, your current CIDP treatment is paused.

This is the most important thing to understand about the study. Before randomization, all participants enter a wash-out phase of up to 12 weeks, during which your current IVIG or other CIDP treatment is paused. This gives the study team a clean starting point to measure how KIg10 affects your condition.

You are not on your own during this time. Symptoms are monitored closely. If your CIDP shows signs of worsening — measured by the INCAT disability score — the wash-out phase ends and active study treatment begins immediately.

Whether this is the right study for you depends on how you and your doctor weigh this pause against the potential benefit of participating. The study team discusses this in detail before you decide.

Who may be a good fit

A few headline criteria below. The screener checks the full eligibility list, and the study team confirms everything before enrollment.

✓

Good fit

  • Adults 18 and older with confirmed CIDPDiagnosed by a neurologist per 2021 EAN/PNS criteria.
  • Currently on CIDP treatmentIVIG, SCIG, corticosteroids, or other standard maintenance therapy.
  • Condition stable for at least 12 weeksNo major symptom worsening or significant treatment dose changes.
  • Weakness in at least two limbsDisability score (INCAT) between 2 and 9, measured by the study team.
  • Open to learning about the wash-out phaseWilling to discuss pausing current treatment for up to 12 weeks before study drug begins.
×

Not eligible right now

  • Pure sensory or multivariant CIDPCertain CIDP variants are not part of this study's design.
  • High-dose IVIG or SCIG requirementsCurrently needing more than 1.3 g/kg/month IVIG or 1.6 g/kg/month SCIG.
  • Prior failure to respond to IVIG/SCIGIf immunoglobulin therapy has not worked previously.
  • Recent immune-modulating therapiesRituximab/alemtuzumab in past 12 months, efgartigimod in past 3 months, certain other agents.
  • Certain medical conditionsBMI > 35, chronic kidney disease, active hepatitis/HIV, recent heart failure, recent blood clots.

What happens step by step

Total participation is approximately 8–10 months. Most infusions take 3–5 hours; assessments are typically 1–2 hours.

1
Today · 3 minutes

Online eligibility check

Answer 5 short questions on this page. If you may be eligible, a study coordinator reaches out within 1–2 business days for a no-obligation phone call to confirm additional details.
2
Weeks 1–4 · 1–2 visits

Medical screening

Visit your assigned study site for a thorough health review: blood work, neurological exam, INCAT disability scoring, and verification of medical history. All study-related costs are covered.
3
Up to 12 weeks

Wash-out phase

Your current CIDP treatment is paused while you're monitored closely. If symptoms worsen (≥1 point on the INCAT score), the wash-out ends early and you transition into active study treatment.
4
21 weeks

Active treatment

KIg10 infusions every 3 weeks at your assigned study site. Disability and symptom assessments at each visit. Both study groups receive active treatment.
5
Final visits

Safety follow-up & close-out

Final assessments at the clinic and a phone check-in 30 days after your last dose. You then transition back to standard care with your regular neurologist.
