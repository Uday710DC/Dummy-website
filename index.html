Frequently asked questions.

A clinical trial is a research study that evaluates a medical intervention — such as a treatment, device, or behavioral approach — in people. Trials follow a written protocol, are reviewed by an Institutional Review Board (IRB), and rely on informed consent before any participant takes part.
Each study has its own inclusion and exclusion criteria. After you complete a brief questionnaire, our platform identifies studies that may fit your profile. Our pre-screening team can help review the basics before a research site makes the final eligibility decision.
Clinical trials follow extensive safety frameworks including IRB review, regulatory oversight, written protocols, and ongoing monitoring. Research teams will explain potential risks and benefits during the informed consent process before you decide whether to participate.
Study-related procedures are typically provided at no cost to participants and many studies do not require insurance. Specific cost details are outlined in the study's informed consent documents.
Yes. Many studies — including early-phase pharmacology and prevention research — specifically need healthy volunteers. Browse our Healthy Volunteers category to see what's currently recruiting.
Many studies offer compensation for participants' time, travel, and study-related expenses. Compensation amounts vary by study and are disclosed up front so you can make an informed decision.
DecenTrialz is HIPAA compliant and ISO 27001 certified. Your information is encrypted in transit and at rest, and is only shared with research sites when you choose to be referred to a specific study.
Our pre-screening team may follow up to confirm a few basics. If you opt in, your information is referred to the research site, which makes the final eligibility decision and handles informed consent.
